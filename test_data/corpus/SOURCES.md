# Test Corpus Sources

This corpus uses public-domain, traditional, or openly licensed text found online for conversion testing.

## Mandarin

- `mandarin/mo_li_hua.txt`: "茉莉花", Wikisource. The page marks the work as public domain because the author died more than 100 years ago and it was published before January 1, 1931. Source: https://zh.wikisource.org/wiki/茉莉花
- `mandarin/xiao_bai_cai.txt`: "小白菜", Wikisource. Chinese Hebei folk song. Source: https://zh.wikisource.org/zh-hans/小白菜
- `mandarin/chile_ge.txt`: "敕勒歌", Wikisource. Northern Dynasties Yuefu folk song; page marks the work as public domain. Source: https://zh.wikisource.org/zh-hant/敕勒歌

## Cantonese

- `cantonese/yueyu_tongyao_selection.txt`: selected Cantonese nursery rhymes from the "粵語童謠" Wikipedia page, available under CC BY-SA 4.0. Source: https://zh.wikipedia.org/zh-hant/粵語童謠
- `cantonese/nanhai_chao.txt`: "南海潮 (歌)", Cantonese Wikipedia, available under CC BY-SA 4.0. Source: https://zh-yue.wikipedia.org/wiki/南海潮_(歌)
- `cantonese/caicha_yue.txt`: Yue-region tea-picking song excerpts from "南越筆記/01", Wikisource. Source: https://zh.wikisource.org/zh-hans/南越筆記/01

## English

- `english/amazing_grace.txt`: "Amazing Grace" from "Olney Hymns (1840)/Book 1/Hymn 41", Wikisource. Source: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Olney_Hymns_(1840)/Book_1/Hymn_41
- `english/twinkle_star.txt`: "The Star" by Jane Taylor, first published in 1806; Wikisource versions page. Source: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Star_(Taylor)
- `english/auld_lang_syne_excerpt.txt`: common English rendering of "Auld Lang Syne"; original by Robert Burns is public domain. Reference source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auld_Lang_Syne
